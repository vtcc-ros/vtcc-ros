C/COL Pramenko’s Command Philosophy

 

I am honored to serve as your Regimental Commander for the Spring Semester of 2019.  I am proud to be part of an organization that is dedicated and willing to become business professionals, or do what less than 0.5% of the population will do—serve and protect our country.

My Command Philosophy serves as the foundation to ensure we complete the Mission of the VTCC which is to graduate leaders of exemplary character who are instilled with the values and skills essential for leadership success in service to the nation.  This guidance provides focus for our team’s development of this exemplary character.

Humility- “There is nothing noble in being superior to your fellow man; true nobility is being superior to your former self.” -Ernest Hemmingway - Humility is not a permanent characteristic. Every day we make decisions that are fueled by a temptation of ego. As leaders we must be aware of our self-centered motives and strive to serve others before ourselves. 

Honesty –Anyone who can be trusted in little matters can also be trusted in important matters. As leaders, we must strive to be honest in every aspect of our life. Leadership is fundamentally based on trust. Without it, the mission will not be accomplished. By telling the truth, subordinates will respect and trust you which will ultimately lift the morale and efficiency of the VTCC.

Hunger - “The capacity to learn is a gift; the ability to learn is a skill; the willingness to learn is a choice.” – Brian Herbert.   Leaders must constantly be developing their professionalism, character, academic knowledge, and skills. Some of this development can be acquired through the VTCC, but whenever possible, strive to become a more “well- rounded” leader. Attend professional development classes, join an academic club, attend a leadership conference, and read books. Each of us is responsible for taking action, getting answers, and making decisions. Don’t wait for someone to help you…seek help and knowledge. Be hungry to become a life-long learner. 

Our priorities are simple; succeed in school, commission or become a business professional, and become leaders of exemplary character.  Not only are we accountable for our own actions, but also for the actions of our peers and subordinates.  We have a duty to uphold the quality and honor of our Virginia Tech Corps of Cadets and each of us must have the courage to do so.

 

Very Respectfully,

Colleen M. Pramenko

Cadet Colonel, VTCC

Regimental Commander, Spring 2019

Understanding the Honor Code

 

In an effort to promote and encourage academic integrity among our students, the Office of Undergraduate Academic Integrity will be sponsoring “Understanding the Virginia Tech Honor Code” sessions. During these interactive sessions students will learn what constitutes an Honor Code violation, various strategies to avoid violations, and campus resources that are available to assist them with their academic work. These sessions are a great way for students to ask questions and gain a better understanding of the honor code in a more personal and relaxed setting.

 

Students are encouraged to attend one of the sessions listed below:

 

Monday, January 28, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Tuesday, January 29, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Wednesday, January 30, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Thursday, January 31, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Friday, February 1, 2019: 6:30 p.m. -8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Disciplinary Hearings

 

An Executive Committee Hearing convened 4 Dec 18 for two freshmen cadets charged with violating VTCC Article 3, Conspiracy; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet, and Article 26, General Article.  The cadets pled Guilty to all charges.  The Committee agreed, stemming from both cadets storing several bottles of their urine in their dorm room for up to two weeks.  The following sanctions were awarded:  verbal/written reprimand; 6 hours BDS; remediation activity completed to the satisfaction of the Company CO.

 

A Performance Review Board / Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 5 Dec 18 for a sophomore cadet charged with violating VTCC Article 4, Absent Without Leave; Article 9, Failure to Obey; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet; and Article 26, General Article.  The cadet pled Guilty to all charges.  The hearing officer agreed, stemming from a pattern of substandard and uncorrected performance.  The following sanctions were awarded:  Dismissal from VTCC with provision to petition to return NET spring 2020 (set aside); 3 weekends restriction; Military Probation for one semester.

 

An Executive Committee Hearing convened 5 Dec 18 for a senior cadet charged with violating VTCC Article 3, Conspiracy; Article 14, Perjury; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet; Article 26, General Article, and Article 29, Visitation Violation.  The cadet pled Guilty to Articles 25 and 26, and Not Guilty to Articles 3, 14, and 29.  The Committee determined Not Guilty of Article 3 and Guilty on all others.  The following sanctions were awarded:  verbal/written reprimand; 6 hours BDS; 3 weekends restriction.

 

A Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 9 Jan 19 for a junior cadet charged with violating VTCC Article 11, Wrongful Appropriation; Article 17, Alcohol Offenses X2; Article 20, Riot or Breach of Peace; Article 23, Assault; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet; and Article 26, General Article.  The cadet pled Guilty to Articles 17, 20, 25, and 26, and Not Guilty to Articles 11 and 23.  The hearing officer agreed with the pleas, stemming from two public alcohol-related incidents while on Study Abroad which brought great discredit to the Corps and the University.  The following sanctions were awarded:  verbal/written reprimand; dismissal from VTCC with provision to petition to return in fall 2019; two letters of apology; upon or before return…educational services provided by Hokie Wellness; two remediation sessions with the Deputy Commandant; reduction to C/CPL rank; Military Probation until graduation; forfeiture of ELS for one semester.

 

A Joint Student Conduct-Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 15 Jan 19 for a sophomore cadet charged with violating several VT policies for abusive conduct, alcohol, and related behavior, and violating VTCC Article 6, Disrespect toward a University Official; Article 7, Assaulting or Willfully Disobeying a University Official; Article 15, Destruction of Property; Article 17, Alcohol Offenses, Article 21, Provoking Speeches or Gestures; Article 23, Assault and Battery; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet, and Article 26, General Article.  The cadet pled Guilty to all.  The VTCC hearing officer agreed, stemming from egregious alcohol consumption, assaulting/battering university and airport officials, racial comments, urinating and vomiting in a sleeping room, and causing disruption to passengers on an international flight, all during an incident while on Study Abroad that brought great discredit to the Corps, University, and the United States.  The following VTCC sanction was awarded:  Immediate dismissal from the Corps with no provision to return.  Separate sanctions from the University included a one-year suspension, pending appeal.
