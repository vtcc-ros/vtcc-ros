Regimental Cadet of the Week

 

C/ Dodge, of Charlie Corps, singlehandedly led Operation Legacy for the VTCC. Operation Legacy is a movement created by the Travis Manion Foundation where groups across the nation get together and perform a community service project in the name of a fallen hero. The service project that C/ Dodge created and executed was a letter writing campaign to send letters to active duty service men and women who are currently deployed overseas. This campaign was in honor of former Charlie Commander Joseph DeWitt. C/ Dodge’s dedication to this service project and reverence for those that lay down their lives for our country positively represents Charlie Corps and the Virginia Tech Corps of Cadets.

 

Honorable Mentions

 

Over Winter Break, C/ Rios served as the coordinator for a cadet volunteer event of laying wreaths on individual grave markers at Arlington National Cemetery. C/ Rios volunteered for this responsibility of her own volition and was dedicated to making sure that all cadets who expressed interest in volunteering were sufficiently informed of all relevant details, taking point in directing the movements of those who volunteered on the day of the event. Cold and pouring rain persisted during the entirety of the event; however, C/ Rios and the 15 cadet volunteers successfully honored those buried at Arlington National Cemetery, including the late Col. Fox. C/ Rios consistently performs exceptionally well as a squad leader and is a well-respected leader who holds herself and her subordinates to the same high standard. C/ Rios has shown her dedication to the University’s motto “Ut Prosim” and Echo Company's motto of "Unity and Pride."

 

C/ Edinger, Echo, for his incredible work ethic and initiative.

 

C/ Mueller, Kilo, for maintaining a high level of morale and doing an outstanding job motivating the entire chain of command.

 

C/ Tinker, India, for exceptional leadership in her position a platoon sergeant.

Understanding the Honor Code

 

In an effort to promote and encourage academic integrity among our students, the Office of Undergraduate Academic Integrity will be sponsoring “Understanding the Virginia Tech Honor Code” sessions. During these interactive sessions students will learn what constitutes an Honor Code violation, various strategies to avoid violations, and campus resources that are available to assist them with their academic work. These sessions are a great way for students to ask questions and gain a better understanding of the honor code in a more personal and relaxed setting.

 

Students are encouraged to attend one of the sessions listed below:

 

Monday, January 28, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Tuesday, January 29, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Wednesday, January 30, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Thursday, January 31, 2019: 6:30 p.m.-8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

Friday, February 1, 2019: 6:30 p.m. -8:00 p.m. in the Graduate Life Center Auditorium

 

Students who plan to attend a session will need to pre-register by emailing honorsys@vt.edu at least one hour prior to the session.
