Special Alumni Panel for All Cadets

 

On Monday, 18 March at 1800, the Corps alumni office is hosting a panel of alumni (recent and older Corps grads) who will discuss their personal view of what is was like to be a “diverse” individual in the Corps.  We have alumni representing several diverse communities- race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.  This will be a very interesting discussion, and we want cadets to attend who want to listen, share, as well as ask questions.  Particularly if you feel treated differently for whatever reason.  Though not mandatory, it is highly encouraged and the Corps company with the highest attendance points will be excused from one morning formation. 

Location is Haymarket Theater (in Squires). 

POC: Col Larkin, Alumni Director, E-87.

Double the Power of Your VTCC Donations During VT Giving Day

 

Giving Day, from 1200 on 19 March to 1200 on 20 March, is a 24-hour, worldwide celebration of Ut Prosim.

 

Seniors who donate $20.19 to any of the Corps of Cadets funds during Giving Day can have their gift count toward the Corps' Giving Day participation goals and toward your class gift.

 

1. Go to givingday.vt.edu and donate $20.19 to any of the Corps of Cadets funds.

2. Mark your Virginia Tech affiliation as “current student.”

3. Include your class year on the next line.

4. Choose “yes” to your donation being your senior class gift.

5. Earn a graduation cord and a special lapel pin.

 

BONUS: Look for the Giving Day cadet challenge. If 25 cadets make a donation while the challenge is active, our alumni will add $2,500 to our totals.

 

If you’ve already made your class gift or you’re not a senior, it only takes $5 to be counted as a Giving Day participant.

Disciplinary Hearings

 

A Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 1 Mar 19 for four (4) junior cadets charged with violating VTCC Articles 9, Failure to Obey; 17, Alcohol Offenses; 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet; and 26, General Article.  The cadets pled Guilty to all charges, stemming from underage consumption of alcohol and/or failing to abstain from drinking alcohol despite written agreement with a university official not to, at a Commonwealth of Virginia legislative function.  One cadet was already on military probation for prior egregious misconduct and was dismissed from VTCC with provision to petition the Commandant to return in spring 2020.  Another cadet had prior alcohol-related incidents in their cadet record and was dismissed from VTCC (sanction set aside).  All involved cadets were reduced in cadet rank, will participate in remediation sessions with their Deputy Commandant or SEA, participate in alcohol-related education services with Hokie Wellness (as applicable), forfeit ELS scholarship (set aside as applicable), and will serve on military probation until graduation from VTCC.
