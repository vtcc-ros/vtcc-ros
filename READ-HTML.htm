Cadet Alumni Staff

 

Do you enjoy networking and meeting VTCC Alumni?  Are you passionate about the Corps of Cadets and the success of the program after you graduate?

 

The Alumni Office is run by Col Larkin (USAF ret. E’87), her assistant Ms. Messner, and this semester’s Cadet Alumni Liaison Officer; C/1LT Mykenzie Webb, Charlie ‘19. Together they work on Alumni engagement and coordinate events such as Corps Reunion, Highty Tighty Reunion, VTCC Alumni Board Meetings, football game flyovers, and the Caldwell Thank-You Letter process.

 

The Alumni Office is looking to put together a permanent Cadet Alumni Staff to help facilitate event preparations, brainstorm event ideas, and assist with Alumni outreach. Cadet members of this staff will be offered great opportunities for networking with Corps Alumni and University Officials and will gain valuable experience assisting in the creation of new initiatives to connect cadets and Alumni. We are primarily looking for Freshmen, Sophomores and Juniors, but Seniors may also apply if interested.

 

Depending on the time of year, this alumni staff would not take a lot of time, and you could still be involved in other organizations/hold leadership positions.

 

Please fill out this survey if you would be interested in joining the staff.

RAM Clinic Volunteering

 

My name is C/Stevens and I am looking for cadets (pre-med have priority) who are interested in taking a volunteer trip to Harrisonburg, VA from March 2-3, 2019 to support a RAM clinic. RAM, or Remote Area Medical, provides free quality healthcare and services to those in need around the United States. RAM depends on the support of the local communities in order to fully function at each clinic location.

 

That is where we come in. The VPI Battalion sent me and a handful of other cadets last semester to Charleston, WV to help support a clinic, and speaking as a medical school hopeful, it really opened my eyes to the dire need for these clinics. Some patients waited for over 24 hours or drove hundreds of miles to have a good spot in line to get treatment that they probably haven't had in years, either due to financial reasons or availability.

 

This semester, with the support of the VPI staff, we are opening up the volunteering opportunity to the entire regiment. Space will be limited, so it will be first come, first serve, with precedence going towards pre-med majors and those who are pursuing some form of medical school education in the future. If you are interested, then please fill out the attached survey NLT COB 06FEB2019.

 

For more information on RAM: https://www.ramusa.org/
