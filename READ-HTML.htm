Special Alumni Panel for All Cadets

 

On Monday, 18 March at 1800, the Corps alumni office is hosting a panel of alumni (recent and older Corps grads) who will discuss their personal view of what is was like to be a “diverse” individual in the Corps.  We have alumni representing several diverse communities- race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.  This will be a very interesting discussion, and we want cadets to attend who want to listen, share, as well as ask questions.  Particularly if you feel treated differently for whatever reason.  Though not mandatory, it is highly encouraged and the Corps company with the highest attendance points will be excused from one morning formation. 

Location is Haymarket Theater (in Squires). 

POC: Col Larkin, Alumni Director, E-87.

2019 Corps Leadership Minor

 

Cadets graduating from the Corps and/or VT this May:

 

If you think you are tracking the Leadership Minor – Corps of Cadets (LMCC), please check your DARS to confirm it is on there. I have had a couple of cadets report that they thought they had declared the minor but had not done so. I want to make sure that if you think you have it that you are correct. If it is not there and you want me to declare it, you should first confirm with your academic advisor that adding a minor at this late date will not have any financial consequences. If you are fine then email me NO LATER THAN Wednesday, March 6, 2019 with your request to declare the LMCC on your behalf.

 

For graduating seniors who had received an email from their advisor/the Registrar about the LMCC being incomplete, please check your DARS. It should show that you have either completed the minor or if you are currently enrolled in one or more required courses that you are on track to complete the minor.

 

Please let me know if you have any questions about this matter.

Dr. Humphrey

Disciplinary Hearings

 

A Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 24 Jan 19 for a sophomore cadet charged with violating VTCC Article 23, Assault; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet, Officer, and Gentleman; and Article 26, General Article.  The cadet pled Guilty to all charges.  The hearing officer agreed, stemming from threatening another cadet verbally and with a weapon.  The following sanction was awarded:  Dismissal from VTCC with no Provision to Return.  University student conduct and legal processes also transpired.

 

A Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 22 Feb 19 for a junior cadet charged with Article 9, Failure to Obey Order or Regulation; Article 17, Alcohol Offenses; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet; and Article 26, General Article.  The cadet pled Guilty to all charges.  The hearing officer agreed, stemming from underage consumption of alcohol and having an unauthorized visitor in the cadet residence hall room after expiration of visitation.  This occurred while the cadet was on Military Probation for previous egregious misconduct during the fall 2018 semester.  The following sanction was awarded: Immediate dismissal from VTCC with provision to petition the Commandant to return no earlier than fall 2019; forfeiture of spring 2019 ELS scholarship.  If granted a return, Military Probation until VTCC graduation; forfeiture of ELS for one semester, forfeiture of ELS all remaining semesters (set aside); reduction in cadet rank, and education and remediation sanctions.

 

Two junior VPI cadets were dismissed from VTCC in the past two weeks for habitual failure to pass Physical Fitness Test standards, without demonstrating sufficient improvement over extended period of time.  This resulted in separate Performance Review Boards to assess overall cadet career performance, which in turn resulted in the recommendation not to retain the cadets in VTCC.

 

A Deputy Commandant Hearing convened 22 Feb 19 for a junior cadet charged with Article 17, Alcohol Offenses; Article 25, Conduct Unbecoming a Cadet, Officer, and Gentleman; Article 26, General Article; Article 28, Fraternization; and Article 29, Visitation Violation.  The cadet pled Guilty to all charges.  The hearing officer agreed, stemming from underage alcohol consumption and multiple instances of inappropriate conduct and unprofessional relationships with subordinate cadets that created an uncomfortable environment and disrupted company good order and discipline.  The following sanctions were awarded:  Dismissal from VTCC (set aside); transfer to another battalion; reduction in cadet rank, forfeiture of ELS scholarship for one semester; referral to Hokie Wellness for alcohol-related education services; 3 weekends restriction; Military Probation for 2 semesters.
