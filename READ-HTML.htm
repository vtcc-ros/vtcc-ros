Relay For Life

 

Good Evening Cadets!

 

Relay For Life at Virginia Tech is a 12-hour event held on April 19th on the Drillfield.  We are raising money for the American Cancer Society.  Symbolic of one's fight against cancer through the darkness, and then finally through light in whatever outcome may result, we host the event from 3pm-3am in honor of one's brutal fight against cancer.  The event involves ceremonies to celebrate and remember our loved ones, as well as field games, musical performances (headliner to be announced soon), unlimited food, and so much more! Contrary to what people may think when they hear "Relay," it is not a race!  Relay involves walking through the night to honor our fighters, and celebrate with our friends and family – it’s fun!  And it's the only night Hokies are allowed to spend an entire night on the Drillfield!

 

A misconception about our event is that when you sign up as a participant, you are committed to attending the event on April 19th - that is not the case!  You do not have to be present at any Relay event to make a difference.  By signing up at www.vtrelay.org, you are already helping us one step closer to a cure, and we appreciate each and every participant. 

 

Did you know that almost every single major cancer breakthrough in the last century involved American Cancer Society sponsorship?  The ACS gives millions and millions of dollars in grants to cancer research each year.  At Virginia Tech, we are the largest fundraising college Relay in the world! 

 

So you've signed up, what now?  The easiest way to raise money on your fundraising page is to share your page to your Facebook. 

See this video on how to link them up!

 

We are so excited that some cadet companies already have teams, and that other companies are in the process of creating one.  Compete against each other!  Who can recruit the most participants, and who can fundraise the most?  You never know... we may have a fun prize for the company that wins! 

 

As always, thank you for your support towards Relay For Life. We look forward to your participation and working together for a world with more birthdays!  Please reach out with any questions to Nicole, Katie, or Zach at director@vtrelay.org.

VTCC Blood Drive

 

The 2nd Virginia Tech Corps of Cadets Blood Drive is coming up! Mark your calendars for April 10th-12th and schedule an appointment with the Red Cross by visiting RedCrossBlood.org or calling 1-800 RED CROSS.

Those who volunteer their time, donate their blood, and spread awareness to the rest of campus truly embody “That I May Serve.”

 

If you have any questions about this upcoming blood drive, please contact C/Jung at caseyj1@vt.edu or (240) 566-2108.

Mae Jemison Keynote

 

 

This is a friendly note from the Women’s Center making you aware of this extraordinary event hosted by Student Affairs and the I WILL Council, financially supported by the Elizabeth A. "Betsy" Flanagan Women in Leadership and Philanthropy Endowed Lecture Fund, and in support of the Women's Center 25th Anniversary!

 

Mae Carol Jemison (born October 17, 1956) is an American engineer, physician and NASA astronaut. She became the first black woman to travel in space when she served as an astronaut aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour. After graduating medical school and a brief general practice, Jemison served in the Peace Corps from 1985 until 1987. In 1987 her application to become an astronaut was accepted by NASA and on September 12, 1992 she was a mission specialist aboard STS-47. In 1993 she resigned from NASA and founded a company researching the application of technology to daily life. She has appeared on television several times, including as an actress in an episode of Star Trek: The Next Generation. She is a dancer and holds nine honorary doctorates in science, engineering, letters, and the humanities. She is the current principal of the 100 Year Starship organization.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The Honeycomb Retreat is a free arts and creative expression-based retreat for young advocates, activists, & movement builders ages 17-22 throughout the state of Virginia. The Honeycomb will be held from July 22-26, 2019 in Richmond, Virginia.

 

Twenty participants (Honeycomb Fellows) from around the state will convene with artists, advocates, activists, and Action Alliance staff to build creative and organizing skills, invest in personal and community healing, and join Virginia's movement against sexual and intimate partner violence. In order to make sure we have state-wide attendance; 10 travel stipends will be available to fellows traveling over 50 miles (one way). 

 

Fellowship applications are currently open! This retreat is intended to center those who have been impacted (both personally and in their community) by sexual violence. We acknowledge that sexual violence disproportionately affects marginalized communities. We strongly encourage people of color, trans and non-binary people, and people who identify as queer, lesbian, gay, and/or bisexual to apply.

 

On the Honeycomb website you'll find more info on logistics, accessibility, and how to propose a workshop!
